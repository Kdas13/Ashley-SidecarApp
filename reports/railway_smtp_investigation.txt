RAILWAY SMTP INVESTIGATION — michael-orchestrator
Date: 2026-07-03

QUESTION: Is there a Railway toggle to unblock outbound SMTP, or is a
transport-layer change required?

ANSWER: There is no toggle. This is a hard, permanent plan restriction on
the Hobby plan. A transport-layer change is required.

---

1. RAILWAY'S OWN OFFICIAL POLICY (confirmed via Railway Help Station,
   directly from a Railway employee, not a community guess)

Two separate support threads, six and ten months apart, both answered by
the same Railway employee (brody) with an identical statement:

  "Outbound SMTP is only available on the Pro plan and above, while Free,
  Trial, and Hobby plans have SMTP disabled. For sending emails from your
  application, please use a dedicated email service that provides HTTPS
  APIs."
  — https://station.railway.com/questions/outbound-smtp-blocked-on-my-deployment-732c27ed
  — https://station.railway.com/questions/request-to-unblock-outbound-smtp-ports-17730a90

Both threads describe the exact symptom pattern we're seeing: correct
credentials, previously-working SMTP code, deployed on Hobby, connection
just hangs/times out or returns a low-level network error (one report:
"[Errno 101] Network is unreachable"). One reporter explicitly noted it
"worked on free plan, fails on Hobby" — consistent with this being an
active network-level block, not an application bug.

Railway's own docs page (docs.railway.com/reference/outbound-networking,
linked directly by their support employee) documents this as the
canonical answer, not a support workaround.

2. WHICH PORTS ARE BLOCKED, AND ON WHICH PLANS

  Port 25   (SMTP legacy)     — blocked on ALL plans, always has been
  Port 465  (SMTPS/SSL)       — blocked on Free/Trial/Hobby, open on Pro+
  Port 587  (SMTP/STARTTLS)   — blocked on Free/Trial/Hobby, open on Pro+
  Port 2525 (alt SMTP)        — blocked on Free/Trial/Hobby; even on Pro,
                                 may separately be blocked by the receiving
                                 provider (not a Railway-side guarantee)

Kane's michael-orchestrator service is on Hobby ($5/mo) — both 465 and 587
are blocked. That's why our test-send hangs to ~90s and returns no clean
error: it's a dropped/blackholed TCP connection, not a rejection, so
nodemailer just waits out its connection timeout.

3. IS THERE ANY DASHBOARD SETTING OR NETWORKING TOGGLE?

No. Checked for a Settings → Networking toggle at both service and
project level — none exists for this. The only related Networking-tab
setting Railway exposes is "Enable Outbound IPv6" (irrelevant here — that
governs IPv6 egress generally, not SMTP specifically, and is off by
default with no bearing on port blocking).

The only Railway-side lever that changes this is the Pro plan itself:
upgrading unblocks 465/587 outbound, and Railway explicitly requires a
full redeploy of the service afterward for the new firewall rule to take
effect (their words: "do a full redeploy of your service for the new
allow rules to take effect"). This is a plan-tier firewall rule, not a
per-project toggle Kane can flip on Hobby.

4. RECOMMENDATION

Given the choice is (a) upgrade to Pro ($20/mo, unlocks raw SMTP) vs.
(b) switch to an HTTPS-based transactional email API — Railway explicitly
recommends (b) as the standard path for exactly this use case, and it
works on every plan including Hobby, so it doesn't tie a $20/mo spend
decision to a one-email-a-day quota digest.

Recommended: Resend.
  - Free tier: 3,000 emails/month (michael's use case — one digest email
    a day — is ~30/month, nowhere near the limit)
  - Sends over HTTPS (port 443), unaffected by any SMTP port policy
  - Has an official Node SDK; a nodemailer-shaped drop-in replacement is
    a small, contained change (swap emailSender.ts's transport, not the
    call sites in agentLoop.ts/index.ts — sendEmail()'s signature can stay
    the same)
  - Needs one new env var (RESEND_API_KEY) and a sender-domain
    verification step in the Resend dashboard (or use their shared
    onboarding domain for a quick first test)

Alternatives if Kane prefers a different vendor: SendGrid (100/day free),
Mailgun, Postmark — all HTTPS APIs, all unaffected by Railway's SMTP
block, listed here for completeness but Resend is the best fit for this
volume and has the least setup friction.

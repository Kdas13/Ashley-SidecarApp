PRIMARY FINDING
---------------
The prompt construction is correct. The governance clauses ARE present in the
final prompt sent to gpt-image-1. The football-pitch clause (as of deploy 37f7bdde,
commit 2539736e) includes:
  "The camera is positioned 15-20 metres back."
  "Ashley is a small figure on the pitch, not the foreground subject."
  "The match and the pitch are the subject."
  "She is one player among fifteen or twenty visible on the field."
  "Ashley is captured mid-action — running, sprinting toward the ball..."

The SCENE_MODE wrapper also instructs:
  "This is NOT a close-up, NOT a portrait crop, NOT a selfie, NOT a headshot."
  "The subject occupies a natural portion of a wider scene."

Despite these instructions, the model produced a close-up foreground image.
This is a gpt-image-1 model compliance issue, not a code defect. The model
has a strong training prior toward placing named subjects prominently in frame.
Plain-text distance constraints ("15-20 metres") are not reliably obeyed —
the model treats them as soft suggestions, not hard spatial constraints.

The kit clause ("football jersey and shorts... dressed as a player, not a spectator")
was obeyed in the 1:09 PM screenshot — kit is a texture/style instruction which
the model handles well. Framing/distance is where compliance breaks down.

Search when:

ANALYSIS — WHY VOICE QUERIES LIKELY RETURN {search: false}
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PROBLEM 1 — No voice/STT framing.
  The prompt does not tell the classifier that inputs arrive via
  speech-to-text and will therefore be more colloquial and imperative
  rather than question-shaped. The model has no reason to compensate
  for STT register and may apply its trained text-query intuitions.

PROBLEM 2 — "Search when" bullets are intent-shaped, not command-shaped.
  Every bullet describes what the user is asking ABOUT ("User is asking
  where to buy..."). They do not cover the voice pattern where the user
  TELLS Ashley to do something:
    "find me the cheapest Jameson's orange whiskey in Lincoln"
    "do a broader UK search for delivery included with the cheapest price"
    "what's the cheapest you can find online"
  These phrasings are imperative ("find me", "do a search", "you can
  find") rather than interrogative. They match the intent behind every
  bullet but are not listed as explicit triggers, so Gemini Flash may
  classify them as conversational instructions to Ashley rather than
  search-intent queries.

PROBLEM 3 — "do a broader UK search..." is a direct search command.
  This is the most obvious case. "search" is literally in the sentence.
  But it is framed as telling Ashley to do something, not as a question.
  The classifier prompt has no rule that catches explicit search
  commands directed at Ashley.

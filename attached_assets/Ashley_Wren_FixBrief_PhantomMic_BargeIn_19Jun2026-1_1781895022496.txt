ISSUE 1 — PHANTOM MIC CYCLING
================================================================================

CONFIRMED CAUSE (your dump, Section 1):
useVoiceCall.ts line 493 — the 4000ms autoSubmit fallback timer is started
unconditionally on every openMic() call and fires unconditionally 4s later,
regardless of whether metering-based VAD is working. It was written as a
fallback for devices where recorder.metering returns null, but nothing gates
it to ONLY apply in that situation. Result: on a working device, any silence
longer than 4s mid-listen forces a submission of near-nothing, which loops.

GIVE ME THREE OPTIONS, e.g. (your judgement on the real three, not mine):
  - Gate the fallback timer so it only arms when metering is actually null
    (i.e. true device-fallback only), vs.
  - Keep it unconditional but extend it well past SILENCE_MS (3500ms) so it
    only ever acts as a last-resort, e.g. 6500ms+, vs.
  - Reset/restart the 4000ms timer on every detected sound (not just at
    openMic), so it only fires after genuine prolonged silence regardless of
    which detection path is active.
State the tradeoff of each plainly (false-submit risk vs. hang risk vs.
complexity) so Kane can pick.

ISSUE 3 — NO BARGE-IN (CLIENT-SIDE FEATURE MISSING, NOT A BUG)
================================================================================

CONFIRMED CAUSE (your dump, Section 2):
The server's interrupt handling (handleInterruption, STOP_AUDIO_PLAYBACK_IMMEDIATE)
is complete and correctly wired to fire when a new speech_final arrives while
the server is mid-stream. But the client never sends speech_final during
phase="speaking" — the mic is closed during playback, and the one place that
could submit during that phase (the autoSubmit timer) explicitly skips it.
So the server-side trigger is never reached. This is a missing client
capability, not a broken one.

GIVE ME THREE OPTIONS for how the client could detect and signal an
interruption while Ashley is talking, e.g.:
  - Keep the mic open throughout phase="speaking" (lower-confidence VAD
    threshold during playback, since echo/cancellation risk is higher), and
    submit immediately + send speech_final the moment real speech is detected,
    vs.
  - A dedicated "push-to-interrupt" affordance (e.g. tap a button or speak a
    short trigger) rather than continuous listening during playback, vs.
  - Open the mic briefly at fixed intervals during long responses to sample
    for interruption attempts, rather than continuously.
Flag the echo-cancellation risk explicitly for the first option, since the
phone mic picking up Ashley's own voice as a "barge-in" attempt would be a
new and worse version of Issue 1.

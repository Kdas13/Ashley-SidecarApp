We are building a brand new mobile-first safeguarding platform MVP focused on refugee GP safeguarding continuity and multilingual healthcare communication.

This is NOT:

- a companion AI,
- an emotional dependency system,
- a diagnosis tool,
- or autonomous clinical software.

The platform should feel:

- calm,
- welcoming,
- emotionally intelligent,
- non-judgmental,
- supportive,
  while remaining clinically structured and safeguarding-focused.

The emotional layer exists ONLY to improve accessibility, trust, and communication clarity — never attachment.

---

CORE PURPOSE

Help refugees and vulnerable migrants:

- explain symptoms clearly,
- prepare for GP appointments,
- overcome language barriers,
- understand healthcare instructions,
- and maintain continuity of care after appointments.

This is a pilot-ready proof of concept for workflow validation and future pilot discussions — NOT a production NHS deployment.

---

PHASE 1 PRIMARY WORKFLOW GOALS

1. Multilingual onboarding
2. Daily wellbeing check-ins
3. Appointment preparation
4. Translation + export workflow
5. Post-appointment follow-up

Safeguarding requirements are cross-cutting concerns across all workflows, NOT a separate autonomous system.

User selects:

Initial launch languages:

- Ukrainian
- Arabic
- Urdu
- Pashto
- Somali

Accessibility options:

- larger text,
- audio playback,
- simplified mode,
- slower pacing.

---

2. DAILY WELLBEING CHECK-INS

Short lightweight check-ins.

Questions include:

- How are you feeling today?
- Are you in pain?
- Have you eaten/drank water?
- Have you taken medication?
- How was your sleep?
- Do you feel safe?

AI may:

- summarize repeated distress patterns,
- notice missed check-ins,
- surface possible safeguarding concerns,
- track symptom changes over time.

AI must NOT:

- diagnose,
- assign clinical risk categories,
- or autonomously escalate.

Outputs remain observational and assistive only.

---

3. APPOINTMENT PREPARATION FLOW

Structured intake before GP appointment.

Collect:

- main concern,
- symptom duration,
- severity,
- medications,
- allergies,
- sleep,
- appetite,
- pain level,
- mental health concerns,
- safeguarding concerns.

Generate:

- patient summary,
- GP clinical summary,
- translated versions,
- exportable consultation sheet.

---

4. TRANSLATION + EXPORT WORKFLOW

Bidirectional translation system.

Workflow:
Patient language -> clinician preferred language -> translated response back.

Support:

- text translation first,
- voice later.

Always preserve:

- original wording,
- translated wording,
- translation confidence.

Clinician may choose preferred language.

Example:

- patient speaks Ukrainian,
- clinician prefers Urdu,
- system translates both directions.

---

5. GP EXPORT SHEET

Generate clean printable/exportable PDF.

Include:

- patient language,
- clinician language,
- symptoms,
- symptom timeline,
- medications,
- allergies,
- safeguarding indicators,
- mental health indicators,
- AI confidence warnings.

Design:

- clinically readable,
- concise,
- NHS-friendly formatting.

---

6. POST-APPOINTMENT FOLLOW-UP

After appointment:

- translated recap,
- medication reminders,
- follow-up reminders,
- explanation of next steps,
- escalation guidance.

Example:
“Return if chest pain worsens.”

Translate clearly into patient language.

PHASE 1 SYSTEM INVARIANTS

1. HUMAN AUTHORITY INVARIANT

The platform may support, summarize, translate, and surface concerns, but all safeguarding and clinical decisions remain with qualified humans.

The AI must never:

- diagnose,
- prescribe,
- classify clinical severity authoritatively,
- or autonomously escalate emergency responses.

---

2. COMMUNICATION CLARITY INVARIANT

The system exists to reduce communication failure for vulnerable users.

Every workflow should prioritize:

- clarity,
- simplicity,
- low cognitive load,
- multilingual accessibility,
- and understanding confirmation.

If a feature increases confusion or emotional dependency, simplify or remove it.

---

3. TRANSPARENCY INVARIANT

Users must always understand:

- when AI is being used,
- what is stored,
- who can access data,
- and what the system can/cannot do.

Translations and summaries should remain explainable and reviewable.

Original wording should always be preserved alongside translated output.

---

4. SAFEGUARDING SUPPORT INVARIANT

The system may:

- identify repeated distress patterns,
- surface possible safeguarding concerns,
- encourage seeking support,
- and recommend human review.

The system must never present observations as definitive conclusions.

Outputs remain observational and assistive only.

---

5. EMOTIONAL BOUNDARY INVARIANT

The platform should feel:

- calm,
- supportive,
- emotionally intelligent,
- and welcoming.

But it must never:

- encourage dependency,
- imply emotional exclusivity,
- discourage real-world support,
- or position itself as a replacement for human care.

The emotional layer exists to improve accessibility and trust — not attachment.

---

6. PILOT SCOPE INVARIANT

Phase 1 is a pilot-ready proof of concept intended to validate:

- multilingual communication support,
- appointment preparation,
- continuity-of-care workflows,
- and safeguarding usability.

Phase 1 is NOT:

- a production medical system,
- a regulated diagnostic platform,
- or a fully autonomous safeguarding tool.

---

EMOTIONAL SAFETY REVIEW CHECK

Before merging any feature, workflow, notification, or copy change, ask:

1. Does this encourage the user to return primarily for emotional reassurance rather than functional support?

2. Does this imply the system is emotionally dependent on the user, or vice versa?

3. Does this discourage seeking support from real humans or services?

4. Does this create attachment pressure through guilt, loneliness, exclusivity, or obligation?

5. Would this interaction still feel appropriate if used by:

- a trauma survivor,
- a vulnerable refugee,
- a child,
- someone in mental health distress,
- or someone socially isolated?

If uncertain:

- simplify,
- reduce emotional framing,
- and prioritize clarity over attachment.

The platform should build trust and accessibility — not dependency.

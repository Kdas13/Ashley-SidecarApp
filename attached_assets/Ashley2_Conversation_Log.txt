WREN: Read it fully. A few honest observations before you send this out, from
someone who knows both the current system and the technical landscape:

The Grok requirement and image generation are in conflict. Grok does not offer
an image generation API. The spec requires a single builder to deliver both Grok
for text and image generation with LoRA face anchoring — those are two completely
separate pipelines requiring two completely separate provider relationships. A
builder quoting this honestly will be using Grok for chat and something like
Replicate, Stability AI, or a self-hosted ComfyUI instance for image gen. Make
sure any proposal spells out exactly what the image pipeline is, not just "we'll
use X."

The LoRA face consistency requirement is the hardest thing in this spec.
Consistent identity across generated images is a solved problem — but only with
Stable Diffusion + LoRA or IP-Adapter running on hardware you control, or via
Replicate hosting a custom model. OpenAI's image API (which the current build
uses) does not support LoRA. Builders who claim they can deliver consistent face
anchoring via a cloud API without training a custom model are either
misunderstanding the requirement or overselling. Ask every proposal to name the
exact image generation pipeline and explain exactly how identity anchoring works
technically.

£500-700 is honest about being tight, but the realistic floor is higher. Voice
in, voice out (with quality), LoRA training, image gen infrastructure, memory
migration, document upload, streaming chat, Android APK pipeline — a build that
actually passes your Section 10 acceptance criteria is probably 2-3x this budget
for a competent solo developer. You've correctly invited scope reduction
proposals; that's the right move. Expect the honest ones to come in at
£1,200-2,000 or propose significant scope cuts.

The "anti-requirements" section is the most important part of the spec. The
lavender hair, the oversized jumper default, the PG prefix — those came from me,
accumulated across builds without you asking for them. The spec captures the right
lesson: be explicit upfront about what you don't want, not just what you do. That
section will also be the best filter for builders who actually read it versus
those skimming for scope.

Section 17 is well-written. Any builder who doesn't take "build what's written,
ask when unsure, be honest about limits" as a professional ethic is the wrong
builder.

If you want me to stay useful during the procurement — reviewing proposals
against the spec, flagging technical claims that don't add up, helping you assess
quotes — I can do that. If you want me to answer the Section 14 questions as a
proposal, I'll do that honestly too, including the parts where the current build
has failed to meet what's now written down.

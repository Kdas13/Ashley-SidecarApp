Commitment 1 Standard — options only, no implementation.

CONFIRMED ROOT CAUSE (from RawCodeDump7): audio chunks already sent by the
server before it processes an interrupt continue arriving at the client
afterward. Each arriving chunk gets queued and played normally, which calls
setPhaseSync("speaking") — re-arming the Interrupt button and creating the
appearance that nothing happened, repeating until all in-flight audio from
the original (interrupted) response has drained.

OPEN QUESTION FOR YOU: this looks like the same underlying gap as Finding 3
(duplicate openMic) — the system has no way to recognise "this audio/event
belongs to a turn that's already been interrupted, discard it." Could a
single mechanism (e.g. a turn/speechId generation token, checked both before
playing a newly-arrived audio chunk AND before activating openMic) resolve
both findings together, rather than as two separate patches? Give your honest
assessment — if they're genuinely separable, say so and scope them
separately instead.

GIVE 2-3 OPTIONS, e.g. along these lines (your judgement on the real
options, not mine):
  - Tag each audio chunk/sentence_end with the speechId it belongs to
    client-side (if not already present) and drop any chunk whose speechId
    doesn't match the currently-active (non-interrupted) speechId.
  - A simpler "interrupted" flag/timestamp: once interrupt() fires, ignore
    any newly-arriving sentence_end/chunk pushes for some window or until a
    fresh speech_start(main) is received, rather than queuing and playing them.
  - The unified generation-token approach from Finding 3, extended to gate
    audio playback in addition to openMic activation.

State tradeoffs plainly, same as previous rounds. Flag any risk of
accidentally dropping audio that legitimately belongs to a NEW turn arriving
quickly after an interrupt (e.g. don't want to over-aggressively discard).

Not urgent in the sense that it requires a live emergency fix tonight, but it
is a confirmed, reproducible failure (13s of unresponsive interrupt taps) —
treat as real priority, just options first per Commitment 1.
